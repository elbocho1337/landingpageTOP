TAPIR

They have a unique prehensile nose, which they use to grasp food, leaves, and branches, and can move it in different directions.
Tapirs communicate with each other through a series of sounds, including chirps, grunts, and whistles.
Baby tapirs are born with a coat of stripes and spots, which helps them blend in with their environment and avoid predators. The coat will gradually disappear as they grow older.
